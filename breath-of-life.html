About the Project

Breath of Life is a mobile breathing game that turns respiratory biofeedback into a playable interaction — using the player's own breath to guide creatures through a living forest, defeat an inner demon, and build lasting habits of emotional self-regulation.

How It Works

01

The player uses their breath as a game controller — inhaling and exhaling to move and interact within the game world.

02

Breathing guides magical forest creatures and confronts an inner demon — a metaphor for the emotional dysregulation the player is working to address.

03

Every breathing exercise is grounded in clinical evidence, drawn from established respiratory biofeedback and emotion regulation research.

Accepted at CHI 2026, this research was designed to support emotion regulation for users in high-stress, isolated environments — a context that demands both clinical rigor and compassionate design. The study focused on women in compulsory isolation drug rehabilitation centers, a population rarely centered in games research.
